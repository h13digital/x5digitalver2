01
"Google Ads is optimizing on roughly 60–80% of your real conversion data — the rest is invisible from Safari and iPhone."
HYPOTHESIS 1 — Tracking is working client-side — and iOS/Safari may be cutting your conversion signal by 20–40%
01
What
we see
GA4 measurement ID G-CGV18PG7XG and Google Ads conversion ID AW-17993580796 are both firing inline via gtag() directly in the page <head> — outside the GTM container. GTM container GTM-WMBLR662 is present but its contents are unverifiable from an external audit. No custom subdomain (e.g. metrics.revin.ai) detected — no server-side tracking endpoint found.
02
Why it
matters

All of Revin's conversion tracking currently runs through the browser — meaning every conversion signal has to exit through the visitor's device before reaching Google or any ad platform. Safari deletes tracking cookies after 7 days. iOS 14+ blocks cross-app tracking by default. uBlock Origin, with 40M+ active users, blocks Google Analytics and Google Tag Manager at the network level.

Revin's ICP — Operations Directors and C-suite at enterprise HVAC and Roofing companies — skews heavily toward Apple ecosystem. Safari is the default browser across that entire segment. These are exactly the buyers whose conversion signals are being cut before they reach Google Ads.

The result: Google's Smart Bidding algorithm is making spend decisions based on a partial dataset — one that systematically underrepresents the ICP you're trying to reach.

02
Why it
matters

Google Ads default attribution is last-click: 100% of conversion credit goes to the final click before a demo booking. In B2B SaaS with multi-week sales cycles, the typical journey looks like: LinkedIn impression → Google Search click → direct visit → demo booked. Under last-click, LinkedIn receives 0% credit even if it planted the initial awareness.

The predictable outcome: LinkedIn appears not to work, budget shifts entirely to Google, LinkedIn is cut. But Google was only capturing buyers LinkedIn had already reached. Demo volume stalls. CPA rises. Nobody knows why.

This is the most common reason B2B SaaS companies underinvest in LinkedIn and then wonder why Google performance deteriorates over time.

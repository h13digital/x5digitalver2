The Hidden Leak

Which makes what we found in the tracking layer more significant — these are competitive advantages worth protecting. Right now, the infrastructure measuring and amplifying them isn't working at full capacity.

4 unconfirmed hypotheses — formed from observable signals only. We flag them because if any one is accurate, it's a meaningful revenue gap.

01
01
What
we see
Two separate tracking plugins — PixelYourSite Pro (v11.2.0.2) and Pixel Manager for WooCommerce (v1.56.0) — are both loaded inline in <head> and both configured to fire against the same Meta Pixel ID 701615693641667. Each plugin generates its own eventID (UUID format, independently generated). There is no GTM client-side container acting as a deduplication layer between them.
02
Why it
matters
Meta's deduplication logic only works when the same event_id arrives from both browser and server-side CAPI within a 48h window. Here, both signals are browser-side, from separate plugin instances, with different eventID values — Meta has no basis to dedup them. Every Purchase event fires twice, reported conversion count in Ads Manager is approximately 2x actual WooCommerce orders, and reported ROAS is approximately 2x the real number. When the duplicate is eventually fixed, platform ROAS will appear to "drop" 30–50%. Performance won't have changed. The measurement will simply be honest for the first time.

Why it
matters
iOS 14+ Intelligent Tracking Prevention and Safari ITP can block the browser JavaScript before that trigger fires. True server-side CAPI (via sGTM) sends the event directly from the server, with no browser involvement — iOS cannot intercept it. An estimated 20–40% of conversions from this demographic may be invisible to Meta's bidding algorithm. The algorithm optimizes on an incomplete signal, bids less aggressively for iOS users, and Cost Per Purchase drifts upward over time.

Which is exactly what makes the five signals below worth flagging.

5 unconfirmed hypotheses — formed from observable signals only. We flag them because if any one is accurate, it represents a meaningful revenue gap. We cannot know without data access. Which is exactly the point.

01
HYPOTHESIS 1 — Conversion data is likely leaking 20–40% every day — and the campaigns don't know it
"Up to 40% of Arlo's iPhone-browsing prospects may be invisible to LinkedIn Ads right now."
01
What
we see
GTM container GTM-TCPWNPZN is confirmed client-side in <head>. LinkedIn Insight Tag (insight.min.js), Meta Pixel (fbevents.js), Microsoft Advertising UET Tag (bat.js), and Quantcast (qevents.js) are detected — either inline or likely firing through the GTM container. No GA4 measurement ID was found inline outside GTM. No server-side tracking subdomain was detected in source code — no metrics.joinarlo.com, gtm.joinarlo.com, or equivalent present.
02
Why it
matters
Every conversion event Arlo sends to LinkedIn and Google today is recorded by JavaScript running inside the visitor's browser. iOS 14+, Safari ITP, and ad blockers intercept those calls and block them before they reach the ad platforms — silently. LinkedIn and Google then calibrate their optimization algorithms on an incomplete dataset, bidding toward audiences that happen to use non-blocking browsers rather than toward Arlo's actual buyers. If 30% of employer prospects are HR Directors or CFOs browsing on iPhone, that entire group is currently invisible to LinkedIn's conversion data. There is no error message.
